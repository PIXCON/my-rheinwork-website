**The brief.** Signal needed a marketing site that spoke fluently to senior engineers, not procurement. The goal was to prove — visually, in seconds — that unifying metrics, logs and traces collapses mean-time-to-resolution, without resorting to generic SaaS gloss.

**The approach.** We built a dark slate canvas with a lime-to-sky accent and set everything in IBM Plex Sans and IBM Plex Mono, so the type itself reads like a console. The page is dashboard-led: a live service-overview mock, a distributed-trace flame graph, an alerts-plus-live-logs split and an incident-response timeline each carry a single, concrete claim instead of marketing copy.

**Why it works.** Engineers trust what they can read off a screen, and every section here shows the actual product surface doing real work. The monospace detailing and restrained accent palette signal precision, while the detect-to-review timeline turns an abstract value prop into a story any on-call team recognises instantly.
