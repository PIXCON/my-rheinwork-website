**The brief.** A 22-seat fine-dining atelier sells an experience, not a menu. The site has to feel like the room — cinematic, still and exclusive — and turn that feeling into reservations, without ever looking like a typical restaurant template.

**The approach.** Matte black and warm ivory with a single muted-gold accent, set in a Cormorant / Fraunces serif, give it the hush of a tasting room. The page is choreographed like the meal itself: a full-bleed hero, the "Kū Journey" menu as an editorial sequence, the chef's philosophy, and a gallery of light and shadow — each scroll a course.

**Why it works.** Every section builds desire and trust — real plating photography, a credible chef story, quiet social proof — and lands on a single, frictionless reservation. It reads like the restaurant itself: confident, considered and quietly expensive.
