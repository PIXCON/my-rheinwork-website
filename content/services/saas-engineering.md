"Minimum viable product" is often misread as an excuse for shortcuts that later turn into months of
cleanup. We see it differently: a good MVP is small in scope but uncompromising in its foundation. We
cut features, not quality — so the first version holds and the next steps build on it.

From day one we factor in what makes a SaaS later: a **multi-tenant architecture** so multiple
customers run cleanly separated on one platform, **billing via Stripe** for subscriptions and
payments, reliable **user management** with roles and permissions, and the **admin interfaces** that
keep your product under control. Technically we rely mainly on Next.js, TypeScript and Postgres.

And we don't just talk about SaaS — we build it ourselves. We know the path from idea to running
product from our own experience, including every lever that matters in practice.
