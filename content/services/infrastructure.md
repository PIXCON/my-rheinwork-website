Software doesn't just have to be built. It has to be operated, and that's where everyday reliability
is decided. We set up your infrastructure cleanly and make sure it still runs quietly a year from
now, without operations turning into a permanent building site.

### Reliability is routine, not luck

A product that constantly wobbles costs trust and revenue. Reliability doesn't happen by chance; it's
the result of disciplined routines: a prepared environment, clear procedures and operations planned
for the worst case. We describe in detail elsewhere how we keep software
[running reliably]({{< relref "/blog/keeping-software-running" >}}) over time. The result is high
availability you can count on.

### Automated deployments lower the risk

Every manual release is a potential source of error. With **CI/CD pipelines**, a risky release
becomes a repeatable, tested procedure. New versions go live faster, with fewer mistakes and the
confidence to roll back cleanly if needed. That takes the pressure off your team with every change.

### Monitoring that only fires on real problems

Good **monitoring** doesn't speak up constantly, but exactly when it matters. We set up clear alerts
that surface genuine issues early, often before your customers even notice. That keeps alerts
meaningful instead of drowning in constant noise, and you stay on top of things without staring at
dashboards all day.

### Only as much technology as needed

Not every project needs a complex cluster. We rely on **cloud setup, Docker and, where it makes
sense, Kubernetes**, but deliberately choose the simplest setup that safely carries your project.
Less complexity means fewer points of failure, lower cost and operations you can still understand
in-house. Hosting is on **EU servers in Germany**.
